M5 Bolt Size and Thread Specifications

Quick answer

M5 bolts measure 5mm major diameter with 0.8mm coarse pitch in standard tables.

They are common in appliances, light structures, and shop jigs.

Use the specifications below and the tap drill calculator whenever pitch differs from coarse.

M5 is a popular metric size for machinery, automotive, and general hardware. With a 5 mm nominal diameter and 0.8 mm pitch, it fits between M4 and M6 in strength and availability. Spanish reference: perno M5. For tap drill size, thread types, and thread identifier workflows, pair this page with tools as you verify threads.

Metric vs UNC thread size

Metric threads list diameter in millimeters and pitch in millimeters per thread; UNC lists nominal inch diameter and threads per inch. They are not interchangeable—taps, dies, and gauges differ.

Read metric vs unc thread context when series labels are unclear.

How to choose the right bolt size

Choosing the correct bolt size depends on load, material, and thread type. Larger diameters increase strength, while thread pitch affects holding force.

FAQ

What is the standard pitch for an M5 bolt?

The standard pitch for M5 is 0.8 mm.

What drill size should be used for tapping M5 threads?

For M5 x 0.8 thread, use a 4.2 mm tap drill.

What imperial screw is closest to M5?

#10-24 is the closest imperial equivalent to M5.
