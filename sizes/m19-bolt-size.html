M19 Bolt Size and Thread Specifications

Quick answer

M19 bolts use 19mm major diameter with 2.5mm coarse pitch in typical references.

They are uncommon but appear between M18 and M20 in some designs.

Use the tables below and thread identifier readings when worn threads confuse sizing.

M19 is rare in general hardware; sourcing taps, dies, and mates may require industrial suppliers. Follow OEM documentation. Spanish reference: perno M19. For tap drill size, thread types, and thread identifier workflows, pair this page with tools as you verify threads.

Metric vs UNC thread size

Metric threads list diameter in millimeters and pitch in millimeters per thread; UNC lists nominal inch diameter and threads per inch. They are not interchangeable—taps, dies, and gauges differ.

Read metric vs unc thread context when series labels are unclear.

Choosing the correct bolt size depends on load, material, and thread type. Larger diameters increase strength, while thread pitch affects holding force.

FAQ

What is the standard pitch for an M19 bolt?

The common coarse pitch for M19 is 2.5 mm. See drawings for fine or special pitches.

What drill size should be used for tapping M19 threads?

For M19 x 2.5 coarse thread, use approximately a 16.5 mm tap drill. Use our Tap Drill Calculator for other pitches.

What imperial fastener is closest to M19?

3/4-10 (approx.) is a practical reference for comparing M19 to inch hardware; verify fit for your application.
