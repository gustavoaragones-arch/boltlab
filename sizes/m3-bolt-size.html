M3 Bolt Size and Thread Specifications

Quick answer

M3 bolts measure 3mm major diameter with 0.5mm coarse pitch in ISO listings.

They fit electronics, cameras, and light assemblies where space is tight.

Use the tables below for tap drill and hex, then the tap drill calculator for non-standard pitches.

M3 is a small metric fastener used in electronics, small machinery, and precision assemblies. It provides a 3 mm nominal diameter with 0.5 mm pitch and is commonly used where space is limited. Metric vs UNC thread size

Metric threads list diameter in millimeters and pitch in millimeters per thread; UNC lists nominal inch diameter and threads per inch. They are not interchangeable—taps, dies, and gauges differ.

Read metric vs unc thread context when series labels are unclear.

How to choose the right bolt size

Choosing the correct bolt size depends on load, material, and thread type. Larger diameters increase strength, while thread pitch affects holding force.

FAQ

What is the standard pitch for an M3 bolt?

The standard pitch for M3 is 0.5 mm.

What drill size should be used for tapping M3 threads?

For M3 x 0.5 thread, use a 2.5 mm tap drill.

What imperial screw is closest to M3?

#4-40 is the closest imperial equivalent to M3.
