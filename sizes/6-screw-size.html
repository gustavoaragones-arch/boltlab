#6 Screw Size and Thread Specifications

#6 is a small numbered (wire gauge) screw size. The most common thread is #6-32 (32 TPI), used in electronics, small assemblies, and general hardware. Diameter is approximately 0.138 in (3.5 mm). Common uses

#6 screws are used in electronics, small machinery, computers, and light hardware. #6-32 is a standard thread for many small machine screws and sheet metal screws. Cross-check loads with the bolt size chart and screw size conversion when you mix metric and inch bins.

Metric vs UNC thread size

Metric threads list diameter in millimeters and pitch in millimeters per thread; UNC lists nominal inch diameter and threads per inch. They are not interchangeable—taps, dies, and gauges differ.

How to choose the right bolt size

Choosing the correct bolt size depends on load, material, and thread type. Larger diameters increase strength, while thread pitch affects holding force.

FAQ

What is #6 screw size?

#6 is a numbered (wire gauge) screw size. #6-32 means a #6 diameter with 32 threads per inch. Diameter is approximately 0.138 in (3.5 mm).

What drill size for #6-32 tap?

Use a #36 drill for #6-32 tapping.

What metric screw is closest to #6?

M3.5 or M4 is the closest metric equivalent to #6 in diameter. See M4 bolt size for metric specs.
